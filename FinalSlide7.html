🧀 Fast Food Grilled Cheeses 🧀


Okay, this may be a bit of a controversial one. Some people really like this sandwich. So much so that they’ve created recipe replications, such as this one.



Something you may have noticed is that the sandwich is made out of potato buns. I actually like this -- with the caveat that the potato buns don’t have sesame seeds. Remember this. This is important for later. The bread is super crisp with a hint of sweetness, but not too sweet, like they used a brioche bun. It’s a bit different from your typical grilled cheese, but this alone isn’t what ruins the sandwich for me.

The cheese. This is the first sandwich where I’ve had a problem with the cheese, and since the cheese is the most crucial part of the sandwich, you’ve got to do it right. From the times I’ve been to Shake Shack, however, the cheese inside the sandwich has never been fully melted. It’s not pull-apart, satisfyingly melted, and instead, you can tell a solid block of cheddar has been put between the potato bun. I understand that sometimes Shake Shack gets busy, but a lopsided grilled cheese, even when paired with a shake and fries, isn’t something I look forward to.



That could just be from my own experience though, as other reviews of the sandwich claim there was TONS of fully melted cheese between their bread. It’s also been claimed that this sandwich was made for vegetarians: not grilled cheese fanatics. As with most restaurants and burger chains on this list, the Shake Shack grilled cheese feels more like an afterthought.

There’s a reason that some call this the worst Shake Shack Sandwich you can get at the chain restaurant. Going for almost $6, you can do much better -- like TWO sandwiches at Sonic.


Overall Rating: 4 out of 10.
